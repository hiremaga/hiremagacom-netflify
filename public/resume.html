How I Work

  • Hold user, business, and engineering in proportion. The best technical decisions come from teams that can hold all three at once: who they’re building for, what makes the business work, and what the system can sustain. The skill is knowing which one to prioritize in the moment, and getting the team aligned on it.
  • Facilitate, don’t direct — except when direction is what’s needed. My default is to create conditions: clear charters, structured risk conversations, pre-mortems, then get out of the way. When a situation calls for decisiveness I can provide that too.
  • Stay technically grounded. Genuine curiosity, not credential maintenance. I follow real questions and build things to test my understanding.
  • Sustainable pace as a default, not a dogma. Teams that rest and focus outperform teams that grind, most of the time. I know when a genuine sprint is warranted and how to call one without burning people out.
  • Learn and share in the open. I build cultures where asking good questions is a sign of strength and reasoning is made visible: in the room, in documents, in how I run a pre-mortem. Occasionally, when something earns a more permanent form, I write at hiremaga.com. The same instinct, different cadences.
