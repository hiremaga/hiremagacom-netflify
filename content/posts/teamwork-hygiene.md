---
title: Hygiene For Teamwork
date: 2026-03-21
---

I spent several years at Pivotal Labs early in my career. The work was XP: test-driven, paired, story-based. It shaped how I think about software delivery more than anything since.

I'm now building a product at Asana heavily inspired by that philosophy. A teammate asked a good question recently: what are the hygiene principles engineers should keep in mind in this new way of working?

Here's what I shared.

**Finish before you start.** Unblocking a teammate beats picking up new work. A story sitting in review is everyone's problem until it's done.

**Stories describe outcomes, not tasks.** If you can't say who benefits, it isn't ready to build.

**Break it down together.** PM, design, and eng decompose stories collaboratively. Small is a skill. A day or two, max.

**Fidelity emerges through conversation.** Alignment isn't prescribed upfront — it develops as you build. Talk early, talk often.

**Done means deployed and verified.** Not merged. Ambiguous done creates shadow work.

**The backlog is alive.** Ungroomed for weeks? Cut it or defer it.

**Velocity belongs to the team.** The moment it becomes a management metric, it gets gamed.

These aren't rules. They're habits that keep flow visible and friction low. The throughline is the same as it was at Pivotal: flow over prediction, visibility over precision.
